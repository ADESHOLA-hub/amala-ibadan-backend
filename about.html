How It Started

Two people, one vision.

Amala Ibadan was established on 13th November, 2020 by Mr. Ifeoluwa Salako and Mrs. Ajibola Salako, with a vision to preserve, promote, and celebrate the rich culinary heritage of Ibadan and the Yoruba people.

What started with fewer than ten staff members, including the founders themselves, has grown into one of the fastest-growing indigenous restaurant brands in Nigeria — built one dish, one guest, and one branch at a time.

What We're Known For

The delight of Amala

Amala is more than just a dish — it's a cherished staple of Yoruba cuisine and a symbol of Nigeria's rich culinary heritage. Made from premium yam flour, it's known for its smooth texture and earthy flavour.

At Amala Ibadan, we serve it the traditional way — alongside authentic soups like Gbegiri, Ewedu, Seafood Okra, and our rich, spicy meat stews. Every bite is stirred to perfection using time-tested methods and only the finest ingredients.

Beyond Amala and other swallow meals, we've grown into a full-service restaurant offering local and continental-inspired favourites — carefully prepared for an unforgettable dining experience.

A Turning Point

Three years in, a partnership changed everything.

After three years of operation, Amala Ibadan entered a strategic partnership with Grocery Bazaar (GB) — accelerating our growth and expansion across Lagos and Ogun States.
